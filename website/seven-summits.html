SAFETY ARCHITECTURE

Built Around
Getting You Home.

Summit success is the goal. Returning safely is the non-negotiable. Every system we operate is designed for that order of priority.

🏔

IFMGA / UIAGM Certified Guides

All lead guides hold the International Federation of Mountain Guides Associations certification — the globally recognized standard for high-altitude guiding competence.

🫁

Supplemental Oxygen Systems

Supplemental oxygen staged at all high camps on Everest and Vinson. Portable altitude chambers (Gamow bags) carried on all expeditions above 5,500m.

🚁

Rescue & Evacuation Coverage

Pre-arranged helicopter evacuation contracts on Everest, Denali, Elbrus, and Kilimanjaro. All climbers carry personal satellite communicators (Garmin inReach).

🩺

Expedition Medic On Every Team

A wilderness medicine certified medic joins every technical expedition. Daily health check protocols, AMS monitoring, and pulse-ox tracking throughout.

🌦

Professional Weather Forecasting

We partner with Meteogroup and local high-altitude specialists for real-time 72-hour summit window forecasting. No summit attempt proceeds without a safe window.

📋

Pre-Expedition Training Audit

12 weeks before each summit, your guide reviews your training log and physical readiness. If we don't believe you're ready, we will delay your summit — for your safety.

EXPEDITION JOURNEY

A 4-Phase
Journey.

Spread across 2027–2028. Each expedition builds on the last. Your guides, support team, and cohort travel the journey with you.

Check Eligibility ↓
Phase 1 · Q1 2027
Africa & Europe — Kilimanjaro + Elbrus

Two attainable high-altitude summits to build team cohesion, acclimatization protocols, and baseline fitness data. Most participants summit Kilimanjaro on Day 8 and Elbrus on Day 20.

Phase 2 · Q2 2027
Americas — Aconcagua + Denali

Technical cold-weather climbing in South America followed by Alaska's demanding expedition-style ascent of Denali. Guides assess each climber's readiness before Phase 3.

Phase 3 · Q3 2027
Oceania & Antarctica — Puncak Jaya + Vinson

Remote jungle approach to Puncak Jaya in Papua, then the frozen continent of Antarctica for Vinson Massif — two of the most logistically complex summits on Earth.

Phase 4 · Spring 2028
Asia — Everest

The final summit. Nepal's spring window, Sherpa support teams, rotational acclimatization to Camp 4, and a summit bid on a confirmed weather window. Completing Seven Summits.

IS THIS FOR YOU?

We Select Across
Three Experience Tiers.

You don't need to have climbed Everest before. But you do need to be honest about where you are — and committed to getting ready.

TIER A — DEVELOPING

Motivated Beginners

No summit experience required for this tier. You need demonstrable physical fitness, a clear training commitment, and a personal statement that shows why Seven Summits is your goal.

  • ✓ Strong aerobic base
  • ✓ Commitment to 18-month training plan
  • ✓ No prior altitude experience required
TIER B — MOST SELECTED ⭐

Intermediate Alpinists

The majority of selected cohort members come from this tier. You've completed at least one technical alpine climb (5,000m+), understand rope work and crampons, and want to reach the next level.

  • ✓ 1+ technical alpine summit above 5,000m
  • ✓ Rope and crampon proficiency
  • ✓ Expedition mindset and team orientation
TIER C — ADVANCED

Experienced Mountaineers

You've summited multiple 6,000m+ peaks, have expedition experience, and are looking for the structured, globally supported framework to complete your Seven Summits systematically.

  • ✓ Multiple 6,000m+ summits
  • ✓ High-altitude expedition experience
  • ✓ Crevasse rescue and self-arrest skills

This Program Is Not For Everyone

We believe in honest selection. We will reject applicants where we don't think it's a fit — for your safety and the integrity of the cohort.

  • Those looking for a bucket-list "tour" without genuine commitment to training
  • Anyone unwilling to follow guide decisions including turnaround calls
  • Applicants without time for a multi-year commitment across all 7 phases
  • Those with unmanaged medical conditions that would affect high-altitude performance
